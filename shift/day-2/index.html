Day 2 of 3

See It.

Yesterday you sat still. Today you're going to look at something most people avoid their whole life. What you actually do when things get hard.

Part 1: Breathe first.

Same thing as yesterday. This takes 90 seconds.

1
3 deep breaths through your nose. As deep as you can.
2
Last exhale: hum until your lungs are completely empty.
3
Sit for 60 seconds. Just like yesterday.

Part 2: Write it out.

Grab a pen and paper. Set a timer for 5 minutes. One question. Don't think. Just write whatever comes out.

"What do I do when I'm stressed?"

Not what you think you should do. What you actually do. Be honest. Nobody's reading this but you.

Most people fall into one of these.

See if you recognize yourself. Most people are a mix.

The Fixer

You take care of everyone. You fix everything. You're the strong one. But you're running on empty and nobody asks if you're okay.

The Busy One

You stay moving. Always doing something. Because if you stop, you might have to feel something you've been avoiding.

The Quiet One

When things get real, you check out. Go quiet. Disappear inside yourself. It's not that you don't care. You care too much.

The People Pleaser

You say yes when you mean no. You bend yourself into shapes to keep people happy. And you lost yourself somewhere along the way.

This isn't a label. It's a pattern. And once you see it, it loses its grip.
